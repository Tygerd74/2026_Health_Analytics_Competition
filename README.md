This project was developed as part of a multi-round healthcare analytics competition (as part of The University of South Carolina (USC) Big Data Health Science Student Case Competition) focused on personalized treatment recommendation and clinical decision support. In the first round, our team analyzed a dataset of 100,000 patient records to estimate individualized treatment effects for surgical versus conservative care across multiple outcomes, including pain reduction, functional improvement, recovery time, and treatment cost. Machine learning and causal inference techniques were used to identify patient characteristics associated with differential treatment responses and generate robust outcome predictions.

In the final round, the challenge expanded to incorporate patient-specific preference weights, requiring treatment recommendations to balance predicted clinical outcomes with individual priorities. We developed a recommendation framework that combined estimated treatment effects with patient utility preferences to generate personalized treatment decisions. The resulting system provided transparent, data-driven recommendations designed to align treatment selection with both expected outcomes and patient-defined measures of value.

The project advanced through multiple competition rounds and ultimately achieved a 7th-place national ranking, demonstrating the effectiveness of the team's predictive modeling and treatment-effect estimation framework.
