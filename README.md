This project was developed as part of a multi-round healthcare analytics competition focused on personalized treatment recommendation and clinical decision support. In the first round, our team analyzed a dataset of 100,000 patient records to estimate individualized treatment effects for surgical versus conservative care across multiple outcomes, including pain reduction, functional improvement, recovery time, and treatment cost. Machine learning and causal inference techniques were used to identify patient characteristics associated with differential treatment responses and generate robust outcome predictions.

In the second round, the challenge expanded to incorporate patient-specific preference weights, requiring treatment recommendations to balance predicted clinical outcomes with individual priorities. We developed a recommendation framework that combined estimated treatment effects with patient utility preferences to generate personalized treatment decisions. The resulting system provided transparent, data-driven recommendations designed to align treatment selection with both expected outcomes and patient-defined measures of value.
